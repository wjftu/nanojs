### Sic Bo Rules

**Sic Bo** (pronounced "see bo") is an ancient Chinese dice game meaning "dice pair" or "dice cube". The game uses three dice and offers many different betting options.

#### Basic Gameplay
- Three dice are rolled simultaneously
- Players can bet on various outcomes
- Winning bets are highlighted after each roll

### Detailed Bet Explanations

#### Small / Big
- **Small**: Wins if the total is 4-10, loses if triples (e.g., 1-1-1, 2-2-2)
- **Big**: Wins if the total is 11-17, loses if triples
- Both pay 1:1 (even money)

#### Triple Bets
- **Any Triple**: Any three of the same number (e.g., 2-2-2 or 5-5-5) → 30:1
- **Specific Triple**: A specific number appears on all three dice → 180:1

#### Double Bets
- **Specific Double**: Two of the three dice show a specific number → 10:1
- Example: Bet on Double 3 wins with 3-3-1, 3-1-3, or 1-3-3

#### Combo Bets
- **Two Dice Combo**: Two specific numbers appear among the three dice → 5:1
- Example: Bet on 3-5 wins with 3-5-1, 3-1-5, 5-3-2, etc.

#### Single Dice Bets
- Bet on a specific number appearing on the dice
- Payout depends on how many dice show that number:
  - 1 die: 1:1
  - 2 dice: 2:1
  - 3 dice: 3:1

### Tips for Beginners

1. **Start with Small/Big**: These have the highest probability of winning (~50%)
2. **Avoid Specific Triples**: The odds are 216:1 against, making this the riskiest bet
3. **Combos are popular**: 5:1 payout with decent odds of winning
4. **Single dice bets**: Good for beginners as you can win multiple times from one roll
